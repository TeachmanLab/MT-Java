What is the Calm Thinking Study?

The purpose of this study is to investigate a new, experimental anxiety reduction program called Calm Thinking. The program consists of the following parts:

  •   One initial assessment
  •   Training Sessions 1-5 (additional assessments following Sessions 3 and 5)
  •   One follow-up assessment

The program runs over the course of five weeks (one Training Session per week) with a two-month follow-up assessment. Each of the assessments and Training Sessions will take approximately 15-20 minutes to complete.



What will I be asked to do?

The assessments consist of a number of questionnaires about your thoughts and mood, your background, and personal mental health history. You will be asked to complete four assessment parts total: prior to Training Session 1, after Training Sessions 3 and 5, and two months following Training Session 5.

In the Training Sessions, you will be presented with one of a few different training programs designed to shift how you think about situations. You may be asked to read information about anxiety problems and their treatment, and answer questions, or read brief stories and complete word fragments, by filling in one or more missing letters in a word. We are testing which program most effectively reduces anxiety.

Although our prediction of which training program will be most effective is based on previous work in the laboratory, it is important to compare the effects of each program in the real world before drawing any conclusions. In psychology research, a common way to compare programs is to randomly assign each participant to one of the training versions.

If you get randomly assigned to a program that we do not think will be the strongest one, you will have the opportunity to complete the program that we expect to be most effective after you finish the study. In other words, EVERYBODY will eventually get the chance to try the program that we expect will be most effective.



What else should I know about participating?

Some participants may be asked to work briefly with a support coach via phone, email, and/or texts, to discuss your progress in the study. After an initial call – which would occur shortly after Training Session 1 – your weekly phone contact with the coach will be brief (typically 2-10 minutes). The coach will not be providing therapy, but can address questions, support your work, and help troubleshoot any issues that that come up when interacting with the online training program.

At the end of the study, you will receive more details about the study’s rationale and design. You will also receive feedback about the progress you made during the program.



How much will I earn by participating?

You will be eligible to receive a $5 electronic gift card for completing the initial assessment and the assessments following Training Sessions 3 and 5. For completing the follow-up assessment, you will be eligible to receive a $10 electronic gift card. Participants will be eligible to receive up to $25 in gift cards (note that values from awarded gift cards can be combined).

Each gift card will be sent via email within 2 business days of being awarded. The cards are submitted through Tango Card, an e-gift cards service through which you can choose from top name-brands like Amazon, Starbucks, iTunes, Target, and many more options; or you can donate the value to a charity.



Are there any benefits or risks to participating?

There may be no direct benefits to you. Depending on the version of the training program that you complete, you may complete procedures that may help reduce symptoms of anxiety.

For some participants, being asked to complete assessments about their thoughts and mood can be temporarily distressing; however, most people experience minimal-to-no distress or do not feel distressed for extended periods of time.

Please note that MindTrails does not provide specific diagnostic or medical advice on our website, and we are limited in how we can respond to your personal requests.

MindTrails is not able to offer financial compensation or to absorb the costs of treatment should you feel you have been injured as a result of participating in the MindTrails research study.



Can I stop at any time?

While we hope that you will complete the entire study, remember that participation in this study is voluntary, and you may end your individual session participation at any time by closing the study window. Further, your participation in this study has no bearing on your standing with other ongoing therapy or employers. Contact the team here if you have any questions about the study or to withdraw completely from the study.

National Institute of Mental Health Data Archive

Data from this study may be submitted to the National Institute of Mental Health Data Archive (NDA). The NDA is a data repository run by the National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) that allows researchers studying mental health and substance use to collect and share deidentified information with each other. A data repository is a large database where information from many studies is stored and managed. Deidentified information means that all personal information about research participants such as name, address, and phone number is removed and replaced with a code number. With an easier way to share, researchers hope to learn new and important things about mental illnesses more quickly than before.

During and after the study, the researchers will send deidentified information about your health and behavior and to NDA. Other researchers nationwide can then file an application with the NIMH to obtain access to your deidentified study data for research purposes. Experts at the NIMH who know how to protect health and science information will look at every request carefully to minimize risks to your privacy.

You may not benefit directly from allowing your information to be shared with NDA. The information provided to NDA may help researchers find better treatments. NIMH will also report to Congress and on its web site about the different studies that researchers are conducting using NDA data. However, you will not be contacted directly about the data you contributed to NDA.

You may decide now or later that you do not want to share your information using NDA. If so, contact us at studyteam@mindtrails.org, and we will tell NDA, which can stop sharing the research information. Please note that NDA cannot take back information that was shared before you changed your mind. If you would like more information about NDA, this is available online at data-archive.nimh.gov.
